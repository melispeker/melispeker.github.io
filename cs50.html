What I Gained From These Courses:

I completed the CS50 Understanding Technology course. I have also completed a few weeks from CS50 Introduction to Computer Science. However, I could not complete the Problem Set assignments since I didn't know how to use GitHub properly. I took notes as I listened to the lectures and did my best to listen carefully.

CS50's Introduction to Computer Science is a Harvard course that teaches the basics of computer science. It covers programming, algorithms, and web development using languages like C and Python. The course includes lectures, projects, and problem sets, making it suitable for beginners and those with some experience. The most important skill this course taught me, in my opinion, was to properly comprehend the idea of bits and bytes. I learned how to translate decimal numbers to binary numbers and vice versa.

CS50's Understanding Technology is a Harvard course that explains how technology works and affects our lives. It talks about the internet, cybersecurity, and media, which are essential knowledge that affects daily life.
